During WW2, Japan committed horrible war crimes
against China and other surrounding
Asian countries.

What happened?

The Japanese military is responsible for using biological warfare, enslaving women into sex work, experimenting
on live prisoners of war, and the Nanking massacre that killed hundreds of thousands of unarmed Chinese soldiers.

Britain.

Nagasaki and Hiroshima

In 1945, atomic bombs were dropped on Nagasaki and Hiroshima by the United States with
support from Canada and Britain. For some, it felt like the right move, but the US government faced backlash
for dropping the bomb on heavily populated areas with critiques claiming that it was a war crime.
The issues commonly brought up by the debate is whether or not Japan deserved it. There are several
things that can be brought to question in regards to the Japanese government and army at this time.
For starters the Nanjing Massacre; the mass killings of Chinese citizens by the Japanese army in 1937,
the treatment of allied POWs; where they were beaten, starved, and abused, and the expiriments taken
place in unit 731; where researchers tested biological warfare on prisoners.

Comfort Women (いあんふ)

The term comfort women was euphemism for women who provided sexual services to Japanese
Imperial Army troops during Japan's militaristic period that ended with World War II. They generally
lived under conditions of sexual slavery and it is recognized as the largest case of government-sponsored
human trafficking and sexual slavery in modern history. Under the 2015 comfort women agreement,
Japan paid 1 billion yen to the comfort women victims. Unfortunatley it ignored more important responsibilities,
such as educating young people or taking measures to prevent secondary harm to victims.

Today

The acts committed by Japan have left a lasting impact on history and are a very important lesson.
They affected millions of people from the Asian countries they invaded, to the western POWs kept in captivity.
In my humble opinion the way the Japanese government glosses over its actions is an indicator
that it doesn't seem to fully accept the consequences of its actions.

All things considered I believe that civilians pay for the mistakes of their leaders and those
mistakes could cost them their lives. What we should all take away from this however is
that ultimatley it is impossible to tell what humanity is capable of when forced to play with
cards at hand.
