Holodomor

Article by: ‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎‎Yuwa Ike

Published Online:‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎‏‏‎ ‎January 25, 2022

Holodomor was a genocide carried out by Joseph Stalin and the
Soviet Union in 1932-1933.

Overview

Holodomor refers to the starvation of millions of Ukrainians in 1932–33 as a result of Soviet policies.
It was the culmination of an assault by the Communist Party and Soviet state on the Ukrainian peasantry, who
resisted Soviet policies. This assault occurred in the context of a campaign of intimidation and arrests of Ukrainian
intellectuals, writers, artists, religious leaders, and political cadres, who were seen as a threat to Soviet ideological
and state-building aspirations.

Stalinism

Stalin targeted Ukrainians by creating a famine that killed millions of people.
He did this by seizing household food, binding farmers from producing food, and
rejecting any possible aid from other countries. Holodomor is a significant part of history.
Millions of Ukrainians were affected by Joseph Stalin and the Soviet Union’s actions

Mimesis

This slideshow presents art that was produced to commemorate the Holodomor over the past 82 years.
The art depicts the tragic demise of the Ukraine people and their suffering through that time period.

Why?

It was all an attempt to force Ukraine into Soviet governance. On the seventh anniversary
of Holodomor, the United Nations issued a statement honouring the lives lost under Stalin's tyranny.
Today we have learned that committing acts of genocide has no place in our society.
